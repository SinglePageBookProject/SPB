What's new in version 1.1?

  • Print to paper support:
    A printed-book-style is now available, useful for first-lectures (start-to-end) approach
    Now you can look at info like a map or like a book with Gutenberg seal-of-approval. To create a printed book just use your browser "Print..." menu.
    Or you can also filter out non-interesting content to create sub-books first.
    Very wide schemas will break, anything else will (hopefully) work.
  • Cleaned Search Interface and keyboard shortcuts now in place.
    Press key "S" to access the search menu (or "/" for vim supremacists)
    Also the Search menu can be open automatically with pre-filled search dialog with pre-selected topics and/or free text.
    Contact with HelpMan for examples.
  • smoother zoom
  • and automatic background coloring of divs, offering a visual help while zoomin in/out
  • Search by text/topics popup can be displayed at page load by just adding the URL parameter '?showSearchMenu'
  • Search Popup now displays the total number of sections found on each topic. This easely servers to evaluate the importance or weight of a given topic on the page/book/map/dashboard/canvas/...(add yours).
  • Simple topic topic-counters are in place.
    Very primitive stat method, but enough to see at a glance what the most content-aware cells are, based on number of topics covered.
    No need to click somewhere of dig into menus to see them, they are where they the deserve, on the front-page.
  • The SPB and IT_notes have been splited in two different projects.
    Historically SPB was just a tool to organized my IT notes, after years of frustration with wikis, online services, note-keeping apps, ... and for more than two years SPB was just extra-stuff around IT_notes. Finally SPB is a own project on itself than can be used for general note-keeping/ordering/searching on its own.
